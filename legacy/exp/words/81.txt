The options are: A. arguing that someone should do something based only on the assertion that it will make him or her feel happier, B. causing confusion during refutation because of a lack of ability to engage in refutation, C. arguing for a conclusion based only on feeling sorry for someone when that feeling is irrelevant to the conclusion, D. arguing someone should do something only because other people will like him or her for doing it. Why the correct answer is: D. arguing someone should do something only because other people will like him or her for doing it?
