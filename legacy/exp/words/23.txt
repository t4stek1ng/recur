There is a multiple-choice question: The access matrix approach to protection has the difficulty that. The options are: A. the matrix, if stored directly, is large and can be clumsy to manage, B. it is not capable of expressing complex protection requirements, C. deciding whether a process has access to a resource is undecidable, D. there is no way to express who has rights to change the access matrix itself. Why the correct answer is: B. it is not capable of expressing complex protection requirements?
