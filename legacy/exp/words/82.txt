There is a multiple-choice question: Which of the following best describes the snob appeal fallacy?. The options are: A. reasoning that a claim is true based only on the evidence that it has been made so often., B. arguing someone should accept a claim based only on the evidence that he or she has the favorable characteristics to understand the truth of the claim., C. arguing a claim should be accepted based only on evidence that unidentified authorities accept it., D. arguing that someone should accept an idea or product based only on evidence that prestigious people accept it, or that non-prestigious people reject it.. Why the correct answer is: C. arguing a claim should be accepted based only on evidence that unidentified authorities accept it.?
