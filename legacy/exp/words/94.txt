There is a multiple-choice question: Which of the following describes the fallacy of appeal to indignation?. The options are: A. attacking the character or background of someone making an argument instead of his or her evidence and reasoning, B. responding to charges of wrongdoing by saying other people do the same thing, C. arguing against a position only based on personal feelings toward the position, D. responding to an argument with an expression of derision instead of a counter argument. Why the correct answer is: D. responding to an argument with an expression of derision instead of a counter argument?
