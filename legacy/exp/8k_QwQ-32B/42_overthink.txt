2. **Work Function (\( \phi \))**: This is the minimum energy required to eject an electron from the metal's surface. It is a **constant property of the material** and does not depend on the incident light's frequency or wavelength.
3. **Stopping Potential (\( V_s \))**: The voltage needed to stop the emitted electrons. The maximum kinetic energy (\( KE_{\text{max}} \)) of electrons is related to the stopping potential by \( KE_{\text{max}} = eV_s \), where \( e \) is the electron charge.
4. **Photoelectric Equation**:  
   \[
   KE_{\text{max}} = hf - \phi
   \]  
   Rearranged to:  
   \[
   V_s = \frac{h}{e}f - \frac{\phi}{e}
   \]
